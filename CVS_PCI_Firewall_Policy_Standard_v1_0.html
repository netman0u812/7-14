PCI Firewall Policy Standard

A Measurable Policy-Function Set for the PCI Cardholder Data Environment  •  Generated 2026-07-15
Purpose and standards basis

This standard defines eight discrete, measurable policy functions for firewall governance across the PCI CDE. Each function is grounded in a specific, currently-active standard — not general best practice — and paired with a formula, a target threshold, and the measurement method needed to track it on an ongoing basis. Baseline figures are drawn directly from the companion Findings document.

Standards referenced, confirmed current as of this writing: PCI DSS v4.0.1 (the only active version, fully mandatory since March 31, 2025); NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 (the current control catalog, 1,196 controls / 20 families); NIST SP 800-41 Rev. 1, Guidelines on Firewalls and Firewall Policy (2009 — still the current revision, no Rev. 2 exists, and the direct NIST source for how a firewall policy itself should be structured); NIST SP 800-30 Rev. 1 for risk rating.

Function 1 — Intrusion Prevention Coverage

1

Every enabled Allow rule in a CDE-adjacent policy carries an active, Prevention-mode IPS policy

Currently Failing
"All network traffic permitted through a PCI-segment firewall must be subject to intrusion prevention inspection operating in blocking (Prevention) mode. Detection-only inspection does not satisfy this function."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 11.4 / Req 6 (general); NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 SI-4 (System Monitoring); NIST SP 800-41 Rev. 1 §5 (firewall/IDPS integration)
Metric
(enabled Allow rules with an IPS policy attached, inspectionMode = PREVENTION) ÷ (total enabled Allow rules), computed per policy and rolled up estate-wide
Target
≥ 95% per policy, no policy below 80%; 0% acceptable in Detection-only mode for any rule in direct CDE scope
Measurement
Automated export of ips_policy / ipsPolicy.inspectionMode fields from FMC per policy, monthly
Owner
Firewall Engineering, reviewed by PCI ISA
Current baseline: 48.0% estate-wide (3,594 of 7,491 enabled Allow rules). 15 of 18 confirmed PCI policies at 0.0%. Where a Prevention-mode equivalent exists at all (the "(IPS Only)" constructs), it runs in Detection mode, which does not meet this function's target as written.

Function 2 — Ruleset Review and Lifecycle Hygiene

2

Every firewall policy is formally reviewed on a fixed cadence, and disabled rules are removed, not accumulated

Currently Failing
"Firewall rulesets are reviewed at least every six months. Rules disabled for longer than one review cycle are removed or formally re-justified, not left in place indefinitely."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 1.2.7 (explicit six-month review cadence); NIST SP 800-41 Rev. 1 §4.3 (ruleset management)
Metric
(disabled rules) ÷ (total rules) per policy; days since last documented review per policy
Target
Disabled-rule ratio ≤ 10% per policy; documented review completed within the last 180 days for every policy, with evidence retained
Measurement
Quarterly ruleset export and review-log audit against the 180-day requirement
Owner
Firewall Engineering, signed off by Change Advisory Board
Current baseline: RETAIL-INTPCI-POLICY at 92.7% disabled (2,686 of 2,898 rules). Middletown/Windsor tier running 4.2%–17.5% disabled per policy. No review-cadence evidence was available to assess against the 180-day target directly — treat as failing until evidence is produced.

Function 3 — Audit Trail Completeness

3

Every enabled rule logs both connection start and connection end, forwarded to a central log destination

Partially Meeting
"Firewall rules record sufficient detail to reconstruct a complete connection lifecycle, and that record is forwarded off-device to centralized, tamper-resistant storage."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 10.2; NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 AU-2, AU-12; NIST SP 800-41 Rev. 1 §4.5 (logging)
Metric
(enabled rules with log_begin = True AND log_end = True) ÷ (total enabled rules); separately, (devices with a confirmed active syslog destination) ÷ (total devices)
Target
≥ 95% dual-logged per policy; 100% of devices confirmed forwarding to a redundant central log destination
Measurement
Monthly rule-field export; annual direct device-config verification of logging host configuration
Owner
Firewall Engineering / SOC
Current baseline: device-level forwarding confirmed active with redundant destinations at every site checked. Dual-logging (start + end) is inconsistent: 21.5% on the master policy set, 92.4% on the Middletown/Windsor tier — neither policy set meets the 95% target uniformly, and the two sets aren't even converging on the same half of the connection lifecycle.

Function 4 — Management-Plane Cryptographic Standards

4

All device management protocols use current, strong authentication and encryption — no legacy fallback

Currently Failing
"SNMP polling uses only version 3 with encrypted authentication. TLS-terminating management interfaces run TLS 1.2 or higher exclusively."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 2.2.1 (no insecure services/protocols) and Req 4.2.1 (strong cryptography); NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 IA-5, SC-8
Metric
(SNMP hosts configured as v3) ÷ (total configured SNMP hosts); (devices with confirmed minimum TLS ≥ 1.2) ÷ (total devices)
Target
100% SNMPv3; zero SNMPv2c community-string hosts; 100% TLS ≥ 1.2 confirmed and documented per device
Measurement
Direct device-config grep for snmp-server host ... version 2c and ssl server-version, per change window and audited annually
Owner
Firewall Engineering
Current baseline: SNMPv2c community-string hosts confirmed present alongside SNMPv3 on PCI devices at every site checked — 0% compliant against the "zero legacy" target. TLS 1.2 confirmed only where the setting was visible in available exports (Woonsocket, GCP) — not yet verified estate-wide.

Function 5 — Rule Specificity and Segmentation

5

No rule permits traffic without specific, justified source, destination, and service scope

Partially Meeting
"Firewall rules are as specific as operationally possible. Any rule using an unrestricted network or port scope carries a documented business or technical justification on file."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 1.2 / 1.3; NIST SP 800-41 Rev. 1 §4.3 (ruleset specificity is a named recommendation)
Metric
count of enabled Allow rules with src_network = any AND dst_network = any; count with dst_port = any, both without an attached justification reference
Target
Zero unscoped rules without a documented, ticketed justification; unscoped protocol-only rules (ICMP, routing protocols) are exempt if formally whitelisted as such
Measurement
Quarterly rule-scope export, cross-referenced against change-ticket records
Owner
Firewall Engineering
Current baseline: 23 any/any rules and 57 any-destination-port rules identified, concentrated in the Legacy VLAN policies. Most any/any instances are protocol-scoped (ICMP, OSPF) and would likely qualify for the protocol-exemption above once formally documented; the any-port subset needs individual review.

Function 6 — Rule Documentation and Categorization

6

Every rule carries a meaningful name, a category, and a traceable change reference

Currently Failing
"No rule exists without a named category and a description sufficient for a reviewer to understand its purpose without cross-referencing external systems."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 1.1 (documented configuration standards); NIST SP 800-41 Rev. 1 §4.3
Metric
(rules with a non-default category) ÷ (total rules); (rules with a name other than an auto-generated migration tag) ÷ (total rules)
Target
≥ 90% categorized; auto-generated migration-tool names (e.g. Tufin-####) reduced to < 5% of the ruleset over time, via re-documentation during each policy's review cycle
Measurement
Annual full-ruleset export and name/category audit
Owner
Firewall Engineering
Current baseline: the category field is unset ("--Undefined--") for 100% of the 10,531 confirmed Tier-1 rules — 0% against target. A confirmed volume of rules across every policy still carry auto-generated migration-tool names rather than descriptive ones.

Function 7 — Policy-to-Device Binding Documentation

7

Every enforcement device has a documented, verifiable mapping to the policy (or policies) governing it

Currently Failing
"For every firewall in the CDE, the specific Access Control Policy and any supplementary inspection policy bound to it is documented and independently verifiable, not inferred from naming convention."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 1.1 (network diagrams and configuration standards); NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 CM-8 (System Component Inventory)
Metric
(devices with a documented, confirmed policy binding) ÷ (total CDE devices)
Target
100% — no device's governing policy should require inference from hostname or directory structure
Measurement
Annual architecture review, reconciling FMC device-registration records against the policy inventory
Owner
Firewall Engineering / Network Architecture
Current baseline: unmet for a specific, material case — whether the "(IPS Only) Las Vegas Voyager" policy binds to the same devices enforcing the five Legacy VLAN policies could not be confirmed from any available export. This function exists specifically to close that class of open question going forward, not just this one instance.

Function 8 — Scope Confirmation for CDE-Adjacent Infrastructure

8

Every policy hosted on CDE-designated management infrastructure has a documented, explicit CDE-scope determination

Currently Failing
"No policy sits in an undetermined scope state. Every access control policy hosted on a PCI-designated FMC manager is explicitly marked as in-scope or out-of-scope for the CDE, with the determination documented and dated."
Standards Basis
PCI DSS v4.0.1 Req 12.5.2 (scoping documentation is an explicit, named requirement); NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 CA-3 (Information Exchange)
Metric
(policies with a documented, dated scope determination) ÷ (total policies on PCI-designated managers)
Target
100% — zero policies in an undetermined state
Measurement
Annual scope review, owned jointly by the PCI ISA and Network Architecture
Owner
PCI ISA
Current baseline: 8 policies, 25,439 rules, hosted on the rricmdc-sec-mgt-prd-pci-01 manager have no documented scope determination available — currently 0% against target. Until this function is met, Functions 1–7 above cannot be fully evaluated for that portion of the estate either.
